<p><p>Microwave dielectric materials are categorized based on their dielectric constants, crucial for applications in microwave technology. Low dielectric constant materials, with values generally below 3, are used in high-frequency circuits to minimize signal loss. Middle dielectric constant materials, ranging from 3 to 10, offer a balance of performance and thermal stability, suitable for various microwave applications. High dielectric constant materials, exceeding 10, are essential for compact devices, enabling miniaturization and improved efficiency in multi-layer circuits and RF components.</p></p>
